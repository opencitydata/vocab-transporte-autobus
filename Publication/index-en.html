Introduction back to ToC

This vocabulary represents the information of the urban bus public transport system. The transport systems of the four cities participating in the Open Cities project have been analysed (A Coruña, Madrid, Santiago de Compostela and Zaragoza) as well as other cities with open data publication policies.

The international standard TransModel that includes the model for the exchange of transport information between companies, operators or transport authorities of the European Union member states has been taken into account. In addition, according to regulation 2017/1926 which follows the European directive 2010 on intelligent transport services ITS (Intelligent Transport System) any authority, operator or company of land transport services must offer their data in formats compatible with Transmodel from December 2019.

During 2019 OEG-UPM has participated in the project SNAP to facilitate the compliance with the legislation through the conversion or generation of GTFS data in NeTeX, the Transmodel format, through the development of the ontology Transmodel, which has been taken into account for the development of this vocabulary.

In general the following Transmodel concepts have been reused: Operator, Authority, Line, Route, Points on a Route, Trip Pattern, Points on a Trip Pattern, Stop, Trip associated to a Trip Pattern, Trip Schedules and Frequencies and Service Calendar. This vocabulary is related to the concept of Incidence in the Traffic vocabulary developed in the context of this project and also to the Postal Address and geosparql vocabularies.

The vocabulary has been divided into three main parts: (1) Operator and its lines, (2) Routes and stops and (3) Journeys

Vocabulary for the representation of data on urban public bus transport: Description back to Index

The vocabulary has been divided into three main parts: (1) Organisations (authority and operator) and their lines, (2) Routes and points thereof, including stops, and (3) Journeys and travel patterns

Figure 1 shows the classes and properties of operators and their lines:

In this portion of the vocabulary, concepts from the Transmodel tmorg and tmjourney models have been reused. First, we have the concept of tmorg:Operator. An Operator can operate tmorg:operating several bus lines tmjourney:Line and each Line may be composed of several routes tmjourney:Route. The esautob:Linea inherits the attributes of the class Line and may be affected by some incidences, esautob:afectadaPorIncidencia. The class estraf:Incidencia belongs to the vocabulary of Traffic. An Operator provides service, tmorg:servingPTFor for an Authority, tmorg:Authority. Both, Operator and Authority, are organisations with Contact Details, tmorg:ContactDetails including some attributes inherited such as telephone or e-mail, among others.

The Figure 2 shows the classes and properties of the routes and stops:

In this portion of the vocabulary, concepts have been reused from the Transmodel tmjourney module. In this module, the general concepts tmjourney:LinkSequence, tmjourney:PointInLinkSequence and tmjourney:Point are managed. A LinkSequence is composed of tmjourney:madeUpOf one or more PointInLinkSequence, which represent the order in a point sequence, therefore the attributes tmjourney:order and tmjourney:distanceFromStart, which represent the distance from the begining of the route, are managed.

Each instance of a PointInLinkSequence is associated to a Point through tmjourney:viewedAs and so a Point is described with a tmjourney:aGenericDescriptionOf by a tmjourney:Place. A Stop esautob:Parada is a Place. The Stop relates to the geosparql pattern for its coordinates and to the Postal Address vocabulary for its address.

A tmjourney:Route is a LinkSequence which is composed of a sequence of points, tmjourney:PontOnRoute and which in turn are associated with points, tmjourney:RoutePoint. In a Route there can be several journey patterns, tmjourney:JourneyPattern which represent their different implementations of a route through the property tmjourney:on; for example, a route may not be fully traveled during certain days of the week. Similar to the route, a travel pattern can be composed of a sequence of points, tmjourney:StopPointInJourneyPattern that are in turn associated with points tmjourney:ScheduledStopPoint, in this case the journey pattern is associated with a destination display tmjourney:DestinationDisplay.

La Figure 3 shows the classes and properties of journeys (at different times and frequencies) that can be made for each journey pattern:

In this part of the vocabulary the central concept is vehicle journey tmjourney:VehicleJourney which are tmjourney:madeUsing a journey pattern. A journey is made up of tmjourney:timedFrom of a sequence of points with planned time recording, tmjourney:TimingPointInJourneyPattern. Each of these elements in this sequence is associated with the class representing the planned times, tmjourney:TimetabledPassingTime. The class esautob:TiempoEsperaReal represents the last waiting time recorded for a point.

The journey is also associated with minimum, maximum and planned frequency information found in the class tmjourney:HeadwayInterval. Each trip is made tmjourney:workedOn according to the parameters of a type of day tmjourney:DayType. There is also a travel schedule, tmjourney:ServiceCalendar which contains the set of day-type assignments.tmjourney:DayTypeAssignment for a period of time. The day type assignments specify tmjourney:specifying one of more day types.

Additionaly in this vocabulary skos schemes have been used to represent the direction of a route tmjourney:orientedBy and the use of a stop through tmjourney:stopUse.

In the Figure 2 are shown several relationships with the SKOS concept class. Each skos:Concept class corresponds to a specific taxonomy and its name is indicated between << >>. For example the property tmjourney:stopUse is related with a skos:Concept that represents the values of the taxonomy tmkosjourney:stop-use-type.

References back to ToC

The urban public bus vocabulary has been aligned with the international standard of TransModel. According to the European regulation 2017/1926 that follows the European directive 2010 on intelligent transport services ITS (Intelligent Transport System) since December 2019, any operator or transport authority must offer its data in formats compatible with Transmodel, short name for the European standard of the reference data model for public transport (EN 12896) made by the CEN (European Committee for Standardization).

In order to facilitate the conversion of NeTEx compatible data from GTFS or its generation from scratch, the OEG has participated in 2018 in a project called SNAP from which the Transmodel ontology has been developed and has been reused in this vocabulary.

In general, the following Transmodel concepts have been reused: Operator, Authority, Line, Route, Points on a Route, Trip Pattern, Points on a Trip Pattern, Stop, Trip associated with a Trip Pattern, Trip Schedules and Frequencies, and Service Calendar.

This vocabulary is related to the concept of Incidence in the Traffic vocabulary developed in the context of this project and also to the Postal Address vocabulary for the location in the street map of the stops and geosparql for its geographical location.
